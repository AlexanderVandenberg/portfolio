Project 1:
Automatic CNC Chip Remover

July 2023 to December 2023

Project Goal: design and build an automatic chip removing system for the Tormach 1100M CNC mill in the Northeastern machine shop

Introduction
At the begining of my capstone project, technicians at Northeastern University's machine shop took 10 minutes a day manually cleaning four CNC milling machines. I spent the next six months with three other engineering students developing a motorized screw auger system that automatically removes chips from a CNC base in under 45 seconds. The final design includes a pully system that fits the size constraints of the CNC base, a trough with coolant drainage holes attached to a pullout drawer, and an on/off operation switch. After creating a successful proof of concept, we handed off the project to the machine shop to implement it in all 4 of Northeastern's CNC machines.

Technical Challenges

  • Needs to be small enough to fit the size constraints of the base of the Tormach 1100M mill
  • Needs to have a long lifespan (more than 5 years) and be easily accessible for maintenance
  • Needs to run directly off of the mill's 24 V power supply
  • Needs to be safe for all users of the Northeastern machine shop

Logistical Challenges

  • Most available screw augers are too wide to fit in the CNC base
  • Limited to the $3000 Capstone budget
  • Must purchase parts with a short enough lead time to come before Prototype Day (December 1st)
